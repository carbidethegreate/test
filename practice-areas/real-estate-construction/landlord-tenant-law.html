Landlord-Tenant Law

Rental agreements create rights and duties for both sides. I help landlords and tenants understand their obligations and resolve disputes effectively.

Alabama’s Uniform Residential Landlord and Tenant Act governs most lease arrangements, setting standards for habitability, security deposits, and eviction procedures. Clear written agreements protect landlords from misunderstandings and give tenants a reliable record of their responsibilities.

When problems arise, quick action prevents further complications. Nonpayment of rent, property damage, and lease violations all require careful documentation. Tenants also have rights when repairs are ignored or utilities are disrupted, and may seek legal remedies if conditions become unsafe.

I assist clients in drafting leases and handling notices, ensuring compliance with statutory timelines. When litigation is unavoidable, my goal is to secure a fair outcome while avoiding unnecessary expense.
