Elder Law

Aging brings unique legal concerns. Elder law covers long-term care planning, asset protection, and guardianship.

Preparing for future medical needs often involves powers of attorney and advance directives. These instruments safeguard decision-making authority when you cannot act for yourself.

Guardianships may become necessary to shield vulnerable adults from exploitation. I work with families to secure appropriate arrangements and monitor fiduciary duties.
